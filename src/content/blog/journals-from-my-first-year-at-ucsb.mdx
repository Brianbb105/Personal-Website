This week is the second week for fall quarter, and all a busy week for me. Since I am working in the DLG dining hall, finding balance between work, study and life had become a big challenge for me. However, I am still working on this and I found that being busy is not a bad thing.
DLG dining hall is a place where I can improve my English and even my Spanish. It was really interesting that those chefs always try to speak with me in Spanish and always say that I look like Mexican instead of other nationalities.
This week, I also restart working out in gym which I usually do in China in order to reduce my stress while I was really stressed out. Recreation Center is the best place to work out thanks to it has large scale area to put various instrument inside. I am doing cardio fitness to reduce my body fat, and I will start doing anaerobic exercise after a while.
That’s all for this week.


<header className="journal-entry-header">
  <h2 className="journal-entry-title">Journal 2</h2>
  <p className="journal-entry-meta">Date created October 15th, 2023</p>
</header>

This week, especially in weekdays, there is nothing really new. I went to school everyday and work at DLG dining hall. I think the highlights of this week is all in weekend.
On Saturday morning, I went to work as usual. While I was reading some articles on social media in the bus, I saw The was going to be an annual solar eclipse in California. That was around 9:14 when the sky suddenly got dark. I went out from the dining hall and I saw that it is getting cloudy outside. All of a sudden, I saw the sun was in a really weird shape, I had reminded of the article that I read that morning. So I took my phone immediately and took some magnificent pictures.

It was really amazing to see this annual solar eclipse, which is a very unusual astronomical phenomenon. After I took these pictures, I sent them to my astronomy professor and he gave me lots of complements.
And on Sunday, Kail and I went to downtown Santa Barbara to be a volunteer for an event called Asian American Neighborhood festival, which is a festival with lots of fun and meaningful activities. In the morning, we set off from Goleta around 7:20 and got there around 7:50. Then we started to move chairs and made tents what made me really tired. However, two hours later, that place came alive. It was really crowded when the event started. As volunteers, Kail and I taught people how to write Chinese calligraphy, which is recognized an art in China. We told people where those characters come from and how to pronounce them etc. I also met bunch of people, which makes me really happy. I met a guy who had had lived in China for few years, he said he had a Chinese name but he forgot it. And I also met a lady, who once was a teacher in China and she lived in Taipei for four year. She can speak really fluent Mandarin and she said she started to learn Spanish these days.
It is a really good and funny weekend. I learned lots of new values and met new people.

The day we left SB, everyone was so excited. We arrived our house booked on Airbnb at 5 PM and then we went to the store to buy some stuff.
In the second day of our trip, we went to the Tunnel View. I knew this place because of the MacOS, which is the computer system for Apple. But when I really see those mountains, I didn’t believe I was really there. And we also went to the waterfall, which is also beautiful. I fell down from the rock because it was rally wet so that I couldn’t stay on it stably. Luckily, it wasn’t that serious. After coming back from our trip, we cooked by ourselves.
